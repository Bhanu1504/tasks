Shivaji

Shivaji I (Shivaji Shahaji Bhonsale; Marathi pronunciation: [ʃiʋaːd͡ʒiˑ bʱoˑs(ə)leˑ]; c. 19 February 1630 – 3 April 1680[5])
was an Indian ruler and a member of the Bhonsle Maratha clan.[6] Shivaji carved out his own independent kingdom from
the declining Adilshahi sultanate of Bijapur that formed the genesis of the Maratha Empire.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Biography - Life History, ...

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj also spelled as Sivaji was born on 19 February 1630. He was born at Shivneri which is a hill fort in
Junnar in Poona, now known as Pune. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj took birth into a family of bureaucrats. His father Shahji
Bhonsale was a great Maratha general in the army of Bijapur Sultanate and his mother Jijabai was a great devotee of religion.
He was the founder of the great Maratha kingdom of India.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Biography - Facts, Life History ...

Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. He is considered to be one of the greatest warriors
of his time and even today, stories of his exploits are narrated as a part of the folklore. With his valor and great administrative skills,
Shivaji carved out an enclave from the declining Adilshahi sultanate of Bijapur.

Shivaji summary

Shivaji , or Sivaji, (born Feb. 19, 1630, or April 1627, Shivner, Pune, India—died April 3, 1680, Rajgarh), Indian king (r. 1674–80),
founder of the Maratha kingdom of India. A devout Hindu, he grew up at a time when India was ruled by Muslims, and he found their
religious persecution intolerable. Collecting a band of followers, he began in c. 1655 to seize weak outposts of the sultan of Bijapur.

Shivaji , or Sivaji, (born Feb. 19, 1630, or April 1627, Shivner, Pune, India—died April 3, 1680, Rajgarh), Indian king (r. 1674–80),
founder of the Maratha kingdom of India. A devout Hindu, he grew up at a time when India was ruled by Muslims, and he found their
religious persecution intolerable. Collecting a band of followers, he began in c. 1655 to seize weak outposts of the sultan of Bijapur.
